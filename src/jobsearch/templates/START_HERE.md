# START HERE — career-hub onboarding

Welcome. career-hub centralizes your professional life so any AI agent
can help you apply for jobs from a single source of truth. This file is
the human-readable guide. The AI version is in [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md) and
[_brain/SESSION_START.md](_brain/SESSION_START.md).

You do **not** fill everything on day one. The system grows with you in
five phases. Each phase has a **goal**, the **files** you touch, a
**success indicator** you can verify, and a rough **duration**.

---

## Phase 0 — Wire the brain (5 min)

**Goal:** any AI agent that opens this folder knows who you are before
asking anything.

**Files:** [_brain/SESSION_START.md](_brain/SESSION_START.md),
[_brain/USER_CONTEXT.md](_brain/USER_CONTEXT.md)

**What to do:**
1. Open `_brain/USER_CONTEXT.md`. Replace every `<placeholder>` in the
   YAML frontmatter (name, languages, location, modality preference,
   salary floor, target sectors).
2. Write a 200-400 word narrative at the bottom — first person, what you
   do at work and what you will not do.

**Success indicator:** open this folder in Claude Code (or any AI
agent), ask *"who am I"*, the AI quotes from your USER_CONTEXT verbatim.

---

## Phase 1 — Your profile is the epicenter (30 min)

**Goal:** at least one fully-populated `PROFILE_<ROLE>.md` so the fit
analyzer has real data to match against.

**Files:**
- [profiles/_template/profile-interview-template.md](profiles/_template/profile-interview-template.md) — the question bank
- `profiles/PROFILE_<ROLE>.md` — the output

**What to do:**
1. Pick the role you target most often (`data`, `meal`, `product`, ...).
2. Open the template in Claude Code and ask: *"interview me using this
   template, pre-filling whatever you already see in USER_CONTEXT."*
3. Walk the 7 sections. The AI should ask only the questions whose
   answer it cannot infer.

**Success indicator:** run `career-hub fit --jd <a-real-jd> --role <role>`
and recognize the matched skills it returns. If 80%+ of the matched list
looks right, your profile is good enough for now.

---

## Phase 2 — Past work in STAR (45-90 min per role)

**Goal:** 2-3 `work_experience/` entries with 5-10 STAR achievements
each. This is the proof the AI quotes in cover letters.

**Files:**
- [work_experience/_template/star-interview-template.md](work_experience/_template/star-interview-template.md)
- `work_experience/<NN>_<slug>/WORK_EXPERIENCE.md`

**What to do:**
- Start with your most recent role. Ask: *"interview me for my World
  Vision role using the STAR template."*
- The AI will push for numbers. If you do not have a number, mark it
  `?` and move on.

**Success indicator:** a cover letter draft generated by `career-hub
apply` quotes at least one real KPI from `work_experience/` without
inventing.

---

## Phase 3 — Your voice on file (20 min)

**Goal:** every AI-written letter sounds like you, not a generic LLM.

**Files:** three templates in `personal_brand/_template/`:
- [brand-discovery-template.md](personal_brand/_template/brand-discovery-template.md) — Skills × Interests × Market needs
- [voice-and-tone-template.md](personal_brand/_template/voice-and-tone-template.md) — always / never rules
- [content-strategy-template.md](personal_brand/_template/content-strategy-template.md) — pillars and cadence

**What to do:** paste 3-5 paragraphs of your own writing into the
voice-and-tone template. The AI extracts patterns and turns them into
prescriptive rules. The discovery and content-strategy templates can
wait if you only care about applications for now.

**Success indicator:** AI-generated cover letter passes a blind test
against your own past writing.

---

## Phase 4 — Custom fit scoring (10 min)

**Goal:** `career-hub fit` reflects what *you* actually weigh, not just
skills and years.

**Files:** [config.toml](config.toml) `[scoring]` block,
[roles/_template/role-criteria-template.md](roles/_template/role-criteria-template.md),
optional `roles/<role>.md` overrides.

**What to do:**
1. Open `config.toml`. Adjust weights in `[scoring.weights]` if the
   defaults do not match how you decide.
2. If you have role-specific filters (e.g. for `data` you accept hybrid
   but not for `meal`), run the role-criteria template and save a
   `roles/<role>.md` with the overrides.

**Success indicator:** `career-hub fit` on a JD that says "must be
onsite in city X" scores lower than the same JD on remote, even when
the skill match is identical.

---

## Phase 5 — Apply, log, learn (ongoing)

**Goal:** every application feeds `_brain/INSIGHTS.md` so the AI gets
smarter about your patterns.

**Files:**
- `applications/<company>/` — auto-generated by `career-hub apply`
- [applications/_template/post-mortem-template.md](applications/_template/post-mortem-template.md)
- `_brain/INSIGHTS.md`

**What to do:** after each application reaches a final status (applied,
interview, offer, rejected, ghosted), run a post-mortem with the AI.
The AI appends one dated entry to `INSIGHTS.md`. Next session, it reads
that entry.

**Success indicator:** after 3 applications, `_brain/INSIGHTS.md` has
3+ dated entries the AI references in your next session ("based on the
IRC post-mortem, lead this letter with the country count").

## Quick reference

| Command | What it does |
|---|---|
| `career-hub init [--data-dir]` | Provision the folder structure |
| `career-hub fit --jd <file> --role <role>` | Score a JD against your profile + filters |
| `career-hub apply --role <role> --company <name>` | Build a full application package |
| `career-hub log --company <name> --status <status>` | Track an application |
| `career-hub report` | Dashboard + skill gap analysis |
| `career-hub scan --profile <role>` | Discover new jobs across ATS portals |

When stuck, open the folder in Claude Code and ask: *"read
`_brain/SESSION_START.md` and tell me what to do next."*

## Tell your shell where your data lives

```
# bash / zsh
export JOBSEARCH_DATA_DIR=$(pwd)

# PowerShell
$env:JOBSEARCH_DATA_DIR = (Get-Location).Path
```

Add this to your shell profile so you do not have to repeat it every
session.
